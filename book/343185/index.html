The Reborn Rebel

A Second Chance at Burning Bright

Preface

On my birthday, I closed my eyes to make a wish. Who knew that right as I opened them, my mom would suddenly shift her body to block me, giving my sister the perfect chance to blow out my candles.

I immediately grabbed the cake and smashed it right into my sister's face.

"Blow, blow, blow! Are you not gonna live to see your own birthday or something?"

Before my sister could even start screaming and crying, I threw myself onto the floor and started crawling around like a dark, twisted creature. I frantically pulled my hair, screamed, cried, did push-ups, paced back and forth, wailed like a ghost, twisted my body in unnatural ways, and rolled my eyes into the back of my head.

My parents were absolutely stunned!

Chapter 2 – The Beating

The aftermath of the cake-smashing incident was, of course, my dad lifting me up by the collar, giving me a huge slap, and then beating the absolute shit out of me.

While getting my ass handed to me, I wailed and cried, freely venting all the grievances I had held in for a lifetime:

"You just favor her! You three are the real family! If that's the case, why didn't you just strangle me when I was born?!"

"Beat me to death! Kill me! I'll reincarnate, you go to prison! In my next life, I'll be your dad, and I'll play favorites too! I'll favor someone else so hard, the moment you are born, I'll flush you down the damn toilet!"

My dad was so furious his veins were popping out like a literal demon.

My mom just looked at me with pure disappointment. "What the hell is wrong with you?"

Joke's on her, her eyes were always full of disappointment anyway. I was immune to it now. I even gave her a smile that clearly said, "I'll fucking do it again."

Chapter 3 – Ground Rules

The people in the compound gave my dad face and didn't blow the incident out of proportion, but everyone was gossiping behind his back.

My dad beat the hell out of me again. This time he was really swinging for the fences.

Right in the middle of the beating, I suddenly blurted out, "Dad, did you forget to eat breakfast? Where's the strength?"

He completely froze. Then his face turned a violent shade of red, like an enraged bull, and he charged at me.

I bolted to the window like a flash of lightning, threw it open, and screamed at the top of my lungs: "Help! Domestic violence! I'm being beaten to death!"

"Beat me to death! Tomorrow you'll be on the front page, and I'll be in hell! I even got the headline ready for you: 'SHOCKING! City Commissioner Does the Unthinkable to His Daughter at Midnight! Is It a Loss of Humanity or Moral Bankruptcy?!'"

My dad forced himself to calm down. "Spit it out. What do you want by acting this crazy?"

"Put the cigarette out," I commanded.

"You little bastard, you dare..."

"Think about your next words carefully before you speak to me."

I crossed my arms, staring down at him with an authoritative glare. "Let's make some ground rules."

Chapter 4 – Demonic Terror

By the time I turned fifteen, I had successfully morphed into the demonic terror of the household.

Sometimes, when Mia tried to snatch my things, Mom would actually snatch them back and snap at her.

One night, Mia came up to me and said, "Sister, I really pity you. What's the point of rebelling against Mom and Dad? You won't get a single red cent of the inheritance in the future."

I hurled the hardcover book straight at her, nailing her right in the forehead.

"Oh my god, Sister! Why did you smash that book into your own head?!"

"If I have committed a sin, let God punish me! Do not hurt my innocent sister!" I roared, then slammed my own forehead into the floor and crawled toward her in a twisted, exorcist-like posture, wiping a booger onto her shirt.

Mia shrieked in terror: "Don't come near me! Stay away!"

Chapter 5 – High School

After middle school, I got admitted into the best high school in the city. Dad wanted to throw a massive graduation banquet, which I instantly vetoed by flipping the dinner table.

"Throw a banquet for your ass."

In my past life, he paraded me around making me toast every middle-aged official with hard liquor. Not this time.

I gave him a high-class, cold smile and started packing my things. My parents suddenly started helping me pack aggressively.

I pulled out a beautifully organized set of study notes with little sticky notes hidden inside — a mini-biography about my dad's inspiring rise from poverty.

My dad got so excited his nostrils flared. "That's my good child!"

Chapter 6 – The Honors Class

Stepping into the honors class, it suddenly hit me: Leo was in this room. Leo Smith. Mia's childhood friend. In my past life, he was my boyfriend who hurt me the deepest.

I immediately walked over and sat down by the garbage cans.

A man who doesn't respect himself is worse than rotten cabbage.

My new desk-mate was Hunter Vance — an absolute edgelord.

When it was time for introductions:

"Greetings. My name is Hunter Vance. My right eye has sealed within it the dark energy of a Demon Lord. My ultimate dream is to defeat the Archangel."

"My name is Chloe. My dream is to become everybody's daddy."

The class erupted. Thanks to Hunter, our nonsense felt wonderfully normal.

Hunter became my desk-mate for all three years. He was weird, delusional, and secretly brilliant.

Chapter 7 – Senior Year

I moved into the school dorms for senior year. My heart belonged solely to studying.

On a rare weekend home, I found my room taken over by Mia's stuff. I lost it.

I grabbed Mia by the hair and delivered two ringing slaps.

Then I saw Leo standing by the doorway in absolute shock.

I immediately called my dad: "Dad! Mia brought a man home!" and snapped a photo.

Later, I smashed Mia's keyboard and took scissors to every plushie.

"From now on, anything of yours that crosses my threshold ends up like this."

Chapter 8 – Graduation

I ranked ninth in the entire state. Hunter was valedictorian.

My parents suddenly treated me like royalty, but I had outgrown their affection.

Dad tried to throw me an 18th birthday party. I told him it sounded terrible.

He finally revealed the truth: Mia was adopted for political capital. I was his only biological daughter.

I called him pathetic and walked away.

Chapter 9 – The Birthday Party

The party went ahead. All the local elites showed up.

Hunter arrived in a razor-sharp silver-grey suit.

"You look really beautiful tonight," he stammered.

We talked. He was flying back to New York the next day and asked me to come with him.

In a dark corner of the ballroom, he dropped to one knee.

"Will you?!"

I hauled him up. "Yes, idiot. I will."

Chapter 10 – Freedom

After the party, I went home to grab my things. Mia was waiting in the dark, pruning a plant with garden shears like a psycho.

She hurled the shears at me in rage. They hit the thick pocket dictionary in my jacket and saved my life.

In the hospital, my mom demanded I sign forgiveness papers for Mia.

I went full goblin mode again and screamed that I couldn't hear her.

The next day, I grabbed my suitcase, gave them a two-finger salute, and said: "Bye."

I walked out into the sunlight. Hunter was waiting by his car, striking a very forced 'cool' pose.

My future was just beginning.

Extra Chapter 2 – Mom (Susan)

I broke my own family to save my ego. I drove my real daughter away. For the rest of my life, I never saw my Chloe again.

Extra Chapter 3 – Hunter

I was the edgy desk-mate who fell hard for the girl who tolerated my Demon Lord nonsense. I successfully abducted her to New York. We got married the second we got our degrees.

Chloe is my angel. Leo can stay mad.

The End

From a broken home to burning brighter than the sun — Chloe Jones
