### Chapter 26

When I opened my eyes, I was blinded by hospital lights. Mom was hovering over my bed.

I scrambled to touch my chest. Wait? No blood? No hole?

Then I remembered. I always kept my thick pocket dictionary shoved in the front pocket of my jacket. I had developed the habit from studying, hoping to crush the Ivy League kids in English vocab.

Told you so: Treat your books well, and in critical moments, they will literally stop a weapon from entering your heart.

"Chloe, you're awake!" Mom cried out, lunging forward. "Quick, Mom brought pen and paper. Sign an affidavit of forgiveness for Mia! Your father had her taken down to the police station!"

See? That's my mother for you.

"Sure. I'll sign the forgiveness papers if you sign a legal document severing our mother-daughter relationship forever," I stared at her with dead eyes.

"How can you be so incredibly selfish?! You're not even injured! She's your sister!" Mom shrieked.

I didn't even dignify her with an answer. I just laid back down.

She grabbed my blankets, violently shaking me, trying to yank me out of bed.

Honestly, it's a bit embarrassing, but I think I had actually fainted from sheer terror. Who wouldn't pass out seeing shears jammed halfway into their sternum?

"Chloe Jones! I raised you! Is this how you repay me?! You absolute ungrateful bitch!"

I shot upright in bed, violently shaking my head, screaming like a lunatic: "I CAN'T HEAR YOU DEAF DEAF DEAF I CAN'T HEAR YOU!"

"You're not even my real mother! No mother on earth would pull this shit when her daughter was almost murdered! You're Mia's mother! Dad said Mia was adopted, but clearly, I'm the adopted one!"

I went full goblin mode again.

"You lie! You're lying! Mia is not adopted! She's my real daughter! I had to hide her in the countryside, but I brought her back when she was three! For ten years we have never been separated! She is my only daughter!" Mom began shrieking like she had finally lost her mind.

What kind of twisted, disgusting karma was this?

### Chapter 27

The next day, I was discharged. Mia was released from custody too.

She had gone completely non-verbal. So had I.

In what should have been the prime of our youth, we had both effectively withered away inside that toxicity.

Richard was at work. It was just the mother and the fake daughter at home.

I grabbed my suitcase, gave them a two-finger salute, and said: "Bye."

Not "see you later," because I was never going to see them again.

I walked out into the sunlight. It was a beautiful day. Hunter was leaning against the hood of a luxury car, striking a very forced, extremely 'cool' pose, waiting for me.

My future was just beginning.

### Extra Chapter 1 (Mia)

When did I first know?

When I was six, I noticed I looked absolutely nothing like my parents. People whispered that I wasn't their real kid.

They looked at me with pity, saying my dad was Richard's subordinate, and I was just an adopted charity case.

Richard always looked at me from a pedestal, dripping in condescension. My mother, however, gave me all the love in the world.

But she was always looking through me, trying to see someone else. When she stared at me, she'd sometimes zone out and mutter the name, "Chloe."

As time passed, she mentioned Chloe less and less. Her eyes were finally filled with genuine love for me.

When I was eight, the real "Chloe" returned.

She was dark, skinny, and tiny. Her lips were cracked, and her hands were rough and calloused.

I was way prettier than her. Mom and Dad definitely had to love me more.

And it was true! All the best snacks and toys went to me. Whatever I wanted, Chloe would painfully hand it over with a look of utter defeat.

Chloe was the spare part. This house didn't want her!

I don't know when that thought dug its roots so deep in my brain. But eventually, Mom began looking at Chloe for longer and longer. Richard's eyes started showing actual fatherly warmth.

Chloe's personality was exactly like Mom's: soft, gentle, and... incredibly easy to bully.

When I was twelve, Chloe suddenly fundamentally snapped. She acted like a rabid dog. I gleefully watched her get beaten bloody by Dad, thinking in my sick head: *Hit her harder! Kill her! I don't want this sister!*

But Dad never crossed that absolute line.

When she turned fifteen, she got into the Ivy League. She was finally leaving. So why did the boy I loved since childhood end up going to the same damn school as her?!

Leo was the only boy who ever looked at me gently.

At Chloe's 18th birthday, I finally confessed my feelings to Leo.

He said: "Mia, you're still young. Focus on school."

I cried: "I will! But please be my boyfriend! I'll study even harder!"

He gently removed my hands. "I only see you as a sister."

I asked if he loved Chloe. He said yes.

He said he loved Chloe's drive, her unhinged fire, her refusal to be invisible.

I clamped onto him sobbing. I knew Leo couldn't resist a victim. If I acted pathetic enough, he wouldn't turn me away.

But he did. He shoved me back and said: "In the beginning, I thought Chloe and I were the same. Quiet, pushovers, always eating shit to keep the peace. But then... she showed me what it looks like to burn brighter than the sun. Now, all I want is to be like her."

When I got home, I completely lost my mind and threw the first thing I grabbed at Chloe. I hadn't realized I was holding heavy steel shears.

Thank God she didn't die.

I despised her, but I didn't want her blood on my hands. I just didn't want her stealing what was mine.

The very next morning, Chloe dragged her suitcase out the door forever.

Richard glared at me like I was radioactive waste. "The minute you turn eighteen, get out of my house."

Overnight, my entire world evaporated.

For the rest of my life, I never saw Chloe again.

At eighteen, I bombed my exams and was physically thrown out on the streets by the only parents I knew. It was then that I tasted true, backbreaking poverty. Years later, I finally understood—I was never fighting for my stuff. I was fighting to keep *her* stuff. I was the parasite. I was the thief.

### Extra Chapter 2 (Mom / Susan)

My name is Susan. I was a poor girl who passed her teaching exams to change my miserable fate.

When I was twenty-three, I met Richard, a kid who had fought his way out of poverty just like me.

We fell in love and had a beautiful little girl.

Richard demanded I quit my job. I refused. I knew the blood and tears my career cost me. I'd give up my life before I gave up my independence.

I wanted to hire a nanny. Richard forbid it. He was a low-level government clerk; people were watching. Hiring a luxury nanny would draw corruption suspicions.

So, we shipped our baby daughter to the rural countryside.

The day we drove away, my baby shrieked and wailed until her lungs gave out.

I never looked back.

*When she grows up, she'll understand the privileges a successful mother can give her,* I told myself.

But shortly after, Richard brought an infant home. He claimed it was his coworker's baby—the parents had died in a horrible wreck. He wanted to adopt it.

I screamed NO. I wasn't even raising my own flesh and blood! Why the hell would I raise a stranger's baby?!

But this time, Richard made a deal with the devil. He said he wanted to launch a corporation, but it was illegal for him to do so. In exchange for raising the kid, he would put me as the CEO and figurehead of the company. It would make me filthy rich.

I didn't understand corporate loopholes, but I knew he needed me alive to run the front.

I agreed. I demanded we bring my real daughter back instantly. He refused. He said the adoption had put him in the political spotlight. If he shipped a kid in from the country now, the optics would be terrible.

"Just hold on a little longer. I'm about to get promoted to City Council," he promised, holding me.

I stared at the strange baby, but my heart was rotting with guilt and grief for my actual child.

Eventually, the guilt broke my mind. I brainwashed myself. I told myself this baby *was* my daughter. My only daughter.

By the time my biological flesh and blood was allowed to come home, my broken brain viewed her as a hostile invader trying to ruin my perfect fantasy.

And finally, I drove my real daughter away. She dragged her suitcase out and said "bye."

A terrible, cold dread settled in my bones. I knew the most precious thing in my life was gone. I wanted to reach out and grab her, but I literally couldn't find a single valid reason in my brain to do it.

They say hindsight is 20/20. When you're in the delusion, you fight the truth to the bitter end.

I broke my own family to save my ego.

I am old now. Chloe wires me an exorbitant amount of alimony every single month. But she has never returned. Richard and I spent years tearing through New York looking for her, but she was a ghost.

Richard and I liquidated all our assets. We kept enough to survive in a dump apartment, and wired millions to Chloe's accounts. All we begged for was five minutes to look at her face.

She didn't take the money. And she didn't want us.

Richard told me his entire life was a cosmic joke. He sold his soul so his kids could stand on top of the world. But in the end, he had no kids left at all.

If he could do it again, he never would have brought Mia home.

If I could do it again, I would have dragged myself through broken glass for my Chloe. I had a million chances to be her mother, and I spat on every single one.

My sweet Chloe.

For the rest of my miserable existence, I never saw my Chloe again.

### Extra Chapter 3 (Hunter)

I was born into New York's elite upper crust. My parents only cared about mergers and acquisitions, meaning I was practically raised by anime and fantasy novels. Thus, I smoothly evolved into an edgy, delusional teen blessed with the Dark Powers of the Underworld.

My parents were so mortified by my behavior they shipped me off to a hyper-strict elite school hundreds of miles away.

And right there, I met the absolute love of my life.

On the very first day, I saw a girl. Adorable from head to toe, checking every single one of my highly specific aesthetic boxes.

But the heart of Lord Hunter Vance is not won so easily!

I haughtily turned my head away.

She calmly pulled out plastic sleeves to wrap her textbooks.

"Only elementary school kids use book covers." My mouth moved way faster than my brain.

I internally screamed in agony and wanted to fling myself off the roof.

She gave me a brutal side-eye.

Using my immense political clout, I demanded she remain my desk-mate for three straight years.

At one point, the teacher moved a random guy next to me. I harassed him so unrelentingly with anime lore that he begged the teacher on his hands and knees to let him move away.

A brilliant tactical victory.

Chloe didn't mind that I was weird, and she didn't mind that my elbows constantly crossed the desk barrier. She didn't even mind when I smelled like absolute ass after basketball practice.

She was a literal angel!

But my angel lived a terrible life. I noticed something. No one ever showed up for her parent-teacher conferences.

My billionaire parents literally chartered a jet from NY to show up for mine, but Chloe had nobody.

She sat alone at her desk, staring into space.

I immediately deployed my mom to become her mom!

My mom winked at me, walked up to Chloe, and said: "Sweetie, how about I pretend to be your mom for the conference? Feel free to call me Mom for practice."

Chloe looked completely stunned, then whispered: "Mom."

I wanted to smash through the window, scale a tree, beat my chest, and roar like Tarzan.

After high school graduation, I successfully abducted Chloe and brought her with me to New York.

The ultimate dream achieved! I was floating on clouds.

The only agonizing part was that Chloe was in med school, and that shit is brutal! I barely got to see her! She was always studying!

Oh, and one more thing. That guy, Leo Smith. Complete prick. Constant annoyance. Why did he keep 'dropping by' the med campus? What was his endgame? That is MY wife, pal.

Don't you have your own wife to bother?

Oh wait, you don't. Loser.

Leo Smith was an absolute menace to society.

Chloe and I got married the second we got our bachelor's degrees. At the wedding, Leo maliciously took terrible, unflattering photos of me.

He texted them to me, saying I looked like a lobotomized golden retriever.

I don't care. I'm a married lobotomized golden retriever.
